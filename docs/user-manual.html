Getting started
Your first five minutes with the Scanner app — what you'll see, what to tap, and how to know you're in the right store.

1 · The five-minute orientation

The Scanner app is the single tool your store uses to flag a problem with a product (wrong price, no barcode, wrong description, etc.) and to tick off your daily checklist. Head Office sees what you log; you see what HO replied. That's the loop.

What you can do

  • Log an HO Task when something needs HO to action it — a wrong price, a barcode that won't scan, a promotion not applying at the till. Nine task types, A through I.
  • Complete a Store Task when HO has assigned your store a daily/weekly checklist item (fridge temp, end-cap photo, opening checklist).
  • Ask other stores on the Product Query board if you find a product with no barcode and want help.
  • Run a Report to see what's been logged today/this week and what HO has come back on.

What you'll see on every screen

  1. A green dot top-right with Store Login · <your store>. If that says the wrong store, sign out and back in.
  2. A side menu on the left: Dashboard, HO Tasks, Store Tasks, Product Query, Reports.
  3. A Sign out button in the top-right corner.
✅
Rule of thumb The top badge is the truth. Always glance at it before recording — every record is filed under the store name shown there.

2 · Signing in & the top badge

How to sign in

  1. Open the app at homesaversscanner.pages.dev (bookmark it).
  2. Username = the 4-digit store code on your name-tag (e.g. 1015).
  3. PIN = the one your Store Manager gave you. First-time PIN is 1234 — change it on day one.
  4. Tap Sign in.
[ screenshot — sign-in form ]
Figure 2.1 · The sign-in screen. The same form is used by every staff member.

Reading the top badge

Once signed in, the top-right of every screen shows two things:

ElementWhat it tells you
Store Login · <Store>You're signed in as a store user, and every record will go to the store named here.
Head Office LoginYou're signed in as an HO/admin user. Only HO accounts show this.
Your display nameThe account label, e.g. Tallaght or Director.
⚠️
If the badge shows the wrong store Sign out and sign back in with the correct username. Don't log records — they would go to the other shop's report.

Changing your PIN

You can't change your own PIN. Ask your Store Manager:

  1. Manager opens Admin → Employees.
  2. Finds your name and taps Reset PIN.
  3. Types your new PIN, saves, and tells you what it is.
🔒
Never share your PIN Everything you do is logged under the signed-in account. Sharing PINs means dispute resolution is impossible.

4 · HO Tasks — the nine task types

An HO Task is a single problem you've spotted with a product. Each task type has its own form designed to capture exactly the information HO needs to fix it once.

Daily task types (A–G)

CodeNameUse it when…
AUOM ErrorsShelf-label unit is wrong (label says "each" but the product is a 6-pack).
BNon-ScansTill can't scan the barcode — beeps red, or shows "item not found". Photo of product + photo of barcode required.
CWrong PricesShelf price and till price disagree.
DWrong DescriptionTill receipt name doesn't match the product (wrong, misspelled, foreign language).
EPrice Marked ProductsProduct packaging shows a printed price different from the till price.
FDRS ErrorsDeposit Return Scheme charge is wrong. Always confirm the return logo is on the product first.
GPromotion ErrorPromotion (2-for-€5, BOGOF, etc.) isn't applying at the till.

One-off task types (H–I)

HStock CountHO-initiated audit — count product on the shop floor.
IMiscellaneousAnything that doesn't fit A–H but is worth flagging.
⚠️
Picking the right task type matters Each task type routes to a different reviewer at HO and surfaces in a different report. A Wrong Price filed as Miscellaneous is much harder to action.

The Quick guide panel

The HO Tasks page has a collapsible Quick guide — daily HO tasks card at the top. It's hidden by default; tap Show to bring up the same table you see above. Use it while training new staff.

5 · Filling an HO Task — step by step

The flow

  1. Side menu → HO Tasks.
  2. Check the Current Store at the top is correct. If you cover multiple shops, pick the right one before scanning.
  3. Pick a Task Type from the dropdown.
  4. Scan or type the Product Code. The description fills in automatically when the product is in our master, and the matched supplier name appears as a note above the form.
  5. Fill in any extra fields the form shows — see the task-specific notes below.
  6. Add a Note if helpful (e.g. "Customer flagged at till").
  7. Tap Save Record.
[ screenshot — Task A form, light theme ]
Figure 5.1 · Task A (UOM Errors) — typical HO Task form with auto-filled description and supplier.

Task-specific bits worth knowing

TaskWhat's special
B Non-ScansTwo photos required — one of the product (label visible), one of the barcode area. UOM and Quantity are optional; only describe what you see.
C Wrong PricesPick a reason code from the dropdown — "shelf-edge label", "shop-floor sticker", etc.
F DRS ErrorsPick the bottle size and how many are in the pack. The app calculates the deposit for you: 6 × 25c = €1.50. Always check the return logo is on the product first.
G Promotion ErrorType the promotion description ("2 for €5") and the price the till actually charged.
📡
If the connection drops You'll see "Saved offline — will sync when you're back online". Keep working. Records send automatically the moment Wi-Fi or 4G is back. Don't log the same record twice.

Editing the supplier

Right under the Product Code the app shows Supplier: B&M Retail Limited (or whoever matched). If that's wrong, the Supplier dropdown immediately below lets you override it without re-typing anything else.

6 · Task statuses: Pending → Completed → Clear

Every HO Task moves through a small set of statuses. Knowing which is which is the most important thing in this app.

StatusWho sets itWhat it means
PendingAuto, on saveYou've raised it. HO hasn't reviewed yet.
Completed by HOHead OfficeHO has actioned the issue (e.g. corrected the price in the system).
No change neededHead OfficeHO reviewed and decided nothing needs changing. A note explaining why is usually attached.
ClearStore, after verifying the fixYou've checked the till/shelf, the fix is live. The record disappears from your list (kept in the database for audit).

The loop in pictures

[ diagram — Pending → Completed by HO → Clear ]
Figure 6.1 · The three-state review loop. The store closes every record once the fix is verified.

How to clear a completed record

  1. Open HO Tasks. Filter by HO completed.
  2. For each row, go to the till/shelf and confirm the fix is in.
  3. Tap ✓ Clear at the end of the row.

The row leaves your active list immediately. Back-office can still find it via Status → Clear (archived) in Reports.

🚦
"Clear" is not "Delete" Clearing only hides the record from working views. The data stays in the database forever. If you want it back, HO can filter by Clear status in Reports.

7 · Store Tasks — the daily checklist

Store Tasks are checklists HO sends to your store on a schedule — daily, weekly, or one-off. They're listed under Store Tasks in the side menu.

[ screenshot — Store Tasks list with To-do and Done sections ]
Figure 7.1 · Today's checklist split into To do and Done today.

Completing a task

  1. Tap a task card under To do.
  2. Read the Instructions at the top (if any). Some tasks include warnings or safety notices.
  3. Answer every required field — these have a red *. Optional fields can be skipped.
  4. Take any photos the task asks for using the camera button.
  5. Tap ✓ Mark complete. The card moves to Done today.

The 19 block types HO can use

Each task is built from a flexible set of blocks. You'll mostly see:

  • Text / Long notes — short answer or comments box.
  • Number, Amount, Percentage, Temperature, Rating — numeric entries with units.
  • Yes/No, Multiple choice, Checkboxes — quick pick controls.
  • Date / Time — pickers with the system keyboard.
  • Signature — type your name to sign off.
  • Photo / File upload — camera capture or document attach.
  • Auto-calculated — a total/average computed from your other numbers; you don't fill it in.
  • Heading / Instruction / Alert / Divider — display-only; nothing to fill.
📷
Photos tip The camera button takes a fresh photo. Choose a well-lit angle, fill the frame with the product/shelf, and check the preview before saving.

8 · Product Query — asking other stores

Sometimes a product turns up at your store with no barcode, an obscure brand, or a returns problem nobody recognises. Product Query is a chain-wide notice board: post a photo, ask a question, any other store can answer.

When to use it

  • You've found a product with no scannable barcode and can't identify it.
  • A customer returned something you didn't recognise, and you want to know if other stores stock it.
  • You need a supplier name and our master doesn't help.
[ screenshot — Product Query feed, light theme ]
Figure 8.1 · The Product Query feed showing open threads with photos and answer counts.

Posting a question

  1. Side menu → Product Query.
  2. Tap + Ask all stores.
  3. Take or upload a photo of the product. This is required.
  4. Write a short note saying what you'd like to know.
  5. Tap Post question.

Answering a question

Anyone signed in can answer.

  1. Open a thread, tap + Add answer.
  2. Type your note. Attach a photo if it helps (optional).
  3. Tap Post answer.

Closing your own thread

When you've got the answer you need, tap ✓ Close thread. Closed threads are hidden from every user — only the asker can close, and there's no way to re-open from the UI (the data is kept for audit if HO ever needs it).

💬
Etiquette Keep the photo focused and the question short. "No barcode — anyone know the supplier?" works better than "help".

9 · Reports — filters, search, and CSV

The Reports page lets you look back at any time range and slice by store, task type, status, or template.

[ screenshot — Reports HO records tab ]
Figure 9.1 · The compact one-line filter row. Multi-select dropdowns mean you can pick several stores or task types at once.

Two tabs

TabWhat it shows
HO recordsEvery HO Task (A–I) with its current status. Default hides Cleared records — change the Status filter to see them.
Store tasksCompleted/pending checklist instances with their answers per template.

Filters

  • From / To — date and time range.
  • Stores — multi-select. Select all, Clear all, search-as-you-type.
  • Task types — multi-select (HO records tab).
  • Status — multi-select (HO records tab). Pick Clear (archived) to look at closed records.
  • Templates — multi-select (Store tasks tab).

Running the report

  1. Set your filters.
  2. Tap Run report.
  3. Use the table that appears: scroll, sort, expand History on any row for the audit trail.
  4. Tap ↓ CSV to download the same data as an Excel-friendly file.
📊
Audit trail Every row in HO records has a ▾ History button. Tap it to see who created the record, who marked it complete, who cleared it, and when. The trail is immutable — once a transition is logged it can't be edited.

Search

There's no global search box. Search is done via the filters: pick the Store / Task type / Status / Template combination you want, run the report, and use your browser's Ctrl+F to find a product code or description in the visible table.

12 · Best practices & do/don't

Do

  • Pick the right task type — the routing at HO depends on it.
  • Add a note if the situation isn't obvious from the data.
  • Take clear photos: well lit, product fills the frame, no blur.
  • Verify and clear completed records on the same day. Don't let them stack up.
  • Glance at the top badge before saving — every single record.

Don't

  • Log the same product twice unless the issue is genuinely different.
  • Sign out while the offline pill is amber.
  • Share your PIN with another staff member.
  • Use Task I (Miscellaneous) when one of A–G actually fits.
  • Treat Clear as Delete — it isn't.

Workflow example: a customer flagged a wrong price

  1. Confirm the price at the shelf vs. the price at the till.
  2. HO Tasks → C — Wrong Prices.
  3. Scan the barcode. Description auto-fills.
  4. Pick the reason code that matches (shelf-edge label / shop-floor sticker).
  5. Type the current shelf and till prices.
  6. Note: "Customer Mary flagged at till 10:32".
  7. Save Record.
  8. Status: Pending. HO will action and flip it to Completed by HO.
  9. Once Completed, check the till is now charging the correct price.
  10. Tap ✓ Clear. Done.

14 · FAQ

Can I edit a record after I've saved it?

No, by design. If something needs correcting, raise a new record with a note explaining what changed. HO will see both, and the audit trail stays intact.

What's the difference between "Completed by HO" and "Clear"?

Completed by HO means HO has actioned the issue. Clear means you've verified at the till that the fix is live. The record only disappears from your list once you tap Clear.

Can I see my closed Product Query threads later?

No — closed threads are hidden from every user. The data is retained in the database for audit but isn't surfaced anywhere in the UI. Only close once you're done.

Why is my UOM warning showing on an Eachs record?

Eachs means a single piece. If the product comes in a pack, every item inside must use the same UOM. Verify pack contents before saving.

I logged a record on the wrong store — what now?

Tell your Store Manager. Back-office can delete the misfiled record from the Reports page. Then re-log it under the correct store.

Does the app work on iPhone / Android / desktop?

All three. It's a website that works in any modern browser; install it as a PWA on iPhone/Android for a faster home-screen launcher.

My scanner gun isn't working in the form.

Click into the Product Code field once (gives it keyboard focus), then scan. The gun behaves as a keyboard, so a focused input is required.

How long are records kept?

Indefinitely by default. Cleared / store-confirmed records can be purged by an admin after 90 days. Photos can be purged after 7 days. Both retention windows are editable by admins.

Can two people use the same login?

Don't. Every action is logged under the signed-in account; sharing a login makes the audit trail meaningless. Each Sales Assistant has their own username and PIN.

Is there a dark mode?

Yes — toggle the moon/sun icon at the top of the screen. This manual itself is light-mode only.

🎓
Last word The app is small on purpose — if a workflow feels like more than 5 steps, ask your Manager whether you're using the right task type. Most jobs are 3 taps.
